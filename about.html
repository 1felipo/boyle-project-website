History of ET N ° 9: "Eng. Luis A. Huergo"

ㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤㅤIn February 1931, the opening of a new industrial school was arranged: the INDUSTRIAL SCHOOL OF THE WEST NATION (EINO); founded by decree of November 24, 1931- and provides it with the peremptory addition of being able to start school courses in the immediate March of 1932. To carry out this mission, Engineer Pedro TORRE BERTUCCI is appointed, who was then vice principal of the OTTO KRAUSE school. A mission arrived in this way was, without a doubt, extremely tough and difficult to accomplish, considering the limited time available, barely two weeks.
ㅤㅤㅤㅤThe elements that it had to carry it out made it even more serious: a number of rooms not very conducive to its new destination; a large house in need of spare parts, a couple of patios limited by a shed; Everything was located in the old Caballito neighborhood, half a block from Rivadavia Avenue, at 67 Georgetown Street, today Juan José Biedma. Transforming that inhospitable construction by giving it a school adaptation, turning it into a living and orderly organization, forming a new industrial school, was the worthy accomplishment of Pedro Torre Bertucci.
ㅤㅤㅤㅤWhat would be the second Industrial School of the Nation had been born in a swift and miraculous realization.
ㅤㅤㅤㅤStill needing some repairs to be able to be fully enabled, the school began its classes on the regulatory date, but with some of the students enrolled, since the rest were sent to the Otto Krause school because many of the classrooms were still under construction.
ㅤㅤㅤㅤThe EINO began operating on April 21, 1932 and did so for a year at the Otto Krause school. She was then authorized to continue classes in the building at 67 Georgetown Street.
ㅤㅤㅤㅤIn 1946, the name “Escuela Industrial de la Nación Oeste” was changed to “Escuela Industrial N ° 2 Ing. Luis Augusto Huergo” (ENHILA). For this, Georgetown Street had also changed its name: it took the name of José Juan BIEDMA in 1939.
ㅤㅤㅤㅤThis school is known among students and teachers by the name of "old Huergo."
ㅤㅤㅤㅤThe President of the Nation, Dr. Humberto Arturo Illia, cedes to the school by decree the land of the old tram workshops located in the block delimited by the streets Martín De Gainza, Franklin, Añasco (today Dr. Nicolás Repetto) and Av Gaona.
ㅤㅤㅤㅤIn this way, the Workshops, Laboratories and practical classrooms sector was inaugurated on 1051 Añasco Street, on November 15, 1969, and the classrooms, administration and other teaching facilities, on November 15, 1978 on Martín Street. by Gainza 1050.
